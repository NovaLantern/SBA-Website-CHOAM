Care, Security, and Maintenance of Rental Equipment

To ensure the longevity and pristine condition of our rental equipment, we ask that all customers adhere to the following care and maintenance guidelines. Proper handling will help prevent damage and minimize the risk of loss:

General Care Instructions:

  • Handle with Care: Always lift equipment, such as tables and chairs, when moving them to avoid scratches or damage to the legs and frames.
  • Protect from Weather: Ensure that items such as wooden chairs, tablecloths, and decorative centerpieces are not left exposed to rain or direct sunlight for extended periods.
  • Clean After Use: Return items in a clean state. Use a gentle cleaner for glassware and tables, and avoid harsh chemicals that may damage surfaces.

Security Tips:

  • Supervise Equipment: During events, designate someone to monitor valuable rental items, such as event lighting, glassware, and silverware.
  • Safe Storage: Store equipment in a secure area when not in use, especially overnight. Lock up smaller items such as cutlery, glassware, and decorative pieces.
  • Avoid Unattended Settings: Never leave high-value equipment, like portable stages and event lighting, in an unsecured location after event hours.

Maintenance Guidelines:

  • Tables and Chairs: Inspect tables and chairs regularly during the event to ensure stability. For folding chairs and tables, confirm that all hinges are securely in place.
  • Fabric Care: Chair covers, sashes, and tablecloths should be spot-cleaned immediately if spills occur. Use non-abrasive cleaners and wash linens on a gentle cycle.
  • Event Lighting: Handle cords and plugs with care to prevent damage. Ensure proper setup by consulting our instructions to avoid electrical issues.
  • Inflatables (e.g., Bouncy Houses): Ensure that the setup area is free from sharp objects and debris. Supervise children at all times to prevent overloading or rough play.
  • Candelabras and Centerpieces: Avoid placing candelabras near flammable materials and use dripless candles when possible to prevent wax buildup. Wipe down centerpieces carefully after use to maintain their appearance.
  • Portable Stages: Ensure proper assembly according to provided instructions. Check for secure connections and avoid overloading with excessive weight.

By following these care and maintenance tips, clients can help keep our rental equipment in excellent condition, ensuring a smooth and successful event while avoiding potential fees for damage or loss.
